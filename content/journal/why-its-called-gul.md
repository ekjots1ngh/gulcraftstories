People ask what *Gul* means, and the honest answer is the simplest one: it means flower. A bloom, a rose, the open face of a marigold. In Urdu, in Persian, in the Hindi I grew up speaking at home, it turns up everywhere, in names, in poems, in the things mothers call their children.

## A house that always had flowers

I cannot remember a single day of my childhood without flowers in the house. Marigolds strung over the door for luck. Rose petals dropped into a steel bowl of water so the front room smelled of them. A garland for a photograph of someone we had lost, replaced before it could wilt.

None of it lasted. That was the point, my mother would say, you make it beautiful *today*, because today is the day you have. I have spent a lot of years quietly disagreeing with her, trying to make a few of those flowers last.

> Gul is flower. Craft is the making. Stories is the part I could never leave out.

## Flower, craft, story

So the name took itself apart and put itself back together. **Gul**, the flower, the bloom I keep trying to hold on to. **Craft**, because everything here is made slowly, by one pair of hands. And **Stories**, because I have never been able to hand someone a piece without telling them where it came from.

That is the whole name, and the whole idea. A flower you can keep. Made by hand. With its story told as carefully as it was made.
