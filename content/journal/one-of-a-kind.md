Here is the one rule I will not bend: every piece I make is one of a kind. There is only ever one. When it sells, it is gone, I do not restock it, and I do not remake it.

People sometimes ask me to. *Could you just do that necklace again, in the same colours?* And I understand why, but the answer is always no, and I want to explain it properly, because it is the heart of what this is.

## Why I won't remake

When I sit down to make something, I am not following a pattern. I am choosing *these* stones, in *this* order, because of how they happened to catch the light that morning. I am glazing a bead and pulling a colour out of the kiln that I could not reproduce if I tried. The piece is a record of one particular day at the bench.

If I made it again, it would be a copy of a feeling, and you would be able to tell. So I would rather make something new.

> One of one, when it's gone, it's gone.

## What it means for you

It means the piece you are holding is the only one in the world. No one will walk into the same room wearing it. It will not turn up, cheaper, somewhere else next season. There is a small, real freedom in that, for you, and honestly for me too.

It also means that if something speaks to you, it is worth listening, because it will not wait. I say that not to rush you, but because it is simply true: the next person who loves it might be quicker.

## And when it's gone

I don't hide the pieces that have sold. They live on in [the Archive](/archive), kept on show like a portfolio, so you can still see the range, the materials, and the way the work keeps changing. Gone, but not forgotten.

That is the promise, start to finish. One piece. One owner. One of a kind.
