When someone picks up a piece and turns over the price, I always wish I could show them the hours folded up inside it. Not to justify anything — just because the hours *are* the piece. They are the part you cannot see, and the part that makes it worth keeping.

## Where the time goes

Take [Meadow Cascade](/shop/meadow-cascade). On paper it is a bib of gemstone chips. In practice it is:

- choosing and sorting dozens of tiny stones by colour
- laying the cascade out so it falls like a real meadow, not a heap
- hand-stitching them to the embroidered band, one at a time
- weighting and balancing the brass drops so the whole thing hangs true

None of those hours are waiting around — they are all hands-on-the-bench. A strung tribal piece like [Caravan Tales](/shop/caravan-tales) is the same story: every coin placed, every tassel tied by hand.

> The hours are the part you cannot see, and the part that makes it worth keeping.

## Why it matters

Handmade has become a word people use loosely, so I want mine to mean something exact: *these specific materials, this pair of hands, this many evenings.* Slow isn't a marketing line — it is simply how long it takes to do properly.

And, quietly, it is the reason each piece can only ever be [one of a kind](/journal/one-of-a-kind). There are only so many evenings.
